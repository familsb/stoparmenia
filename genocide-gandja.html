Gandja Terror

The Ganja ballistic missile attacks were four separate missile attacks on the city of Ganja, Azerbaijan in October 2020, during the 2020 Nagorno-Karabakh conflict. Ganja was first hit by an OTR-21 Tochka ballistic missile on 4 October. Azerbaijan accused Armenia of the attack, which it denied, with Artsakh taking responsibility, stating that it fired on military targets, especially Ganja International Airport, while Azerbaijan stated that there were no military targets in the city. Subsequently, both a correspondent reporting from the scene for a Russian media outlet and the airport director denied that the airport, which was not operational since March due to the COVID-19 pandemic, had been hit. As a result of the attack, one civilian was killed, while 30 were injured. It was one of the first serious attacks in the conflict outside the Nagorno-Karabakh region. Ganja was again hit on 8 October. No civilian casualties were reported, but a number of residential buildings and one private vehicle were damaged. A day after the ceasefire signed on 10 October, at 02:00 local time the Azerbaijani Ministry of Defence stated that units belonging to the Armenian Armed Forces in Berd, Armenia, had fired upon Ganja with a Scud missile. The missile hit an apartment building, completely destroying it. Shortly after, search and rescue teams arrived at the scene. During the attack, significant infrastructure in the city namely 31 apartment buildings were completely destroyed, structures and vehicles in the vicinity of the explosion were seriously damaged. The attack affected 205 people living in a total of 95 apartments. Armenia denied responsibility for the attack. As a result of the attack, ten people were killed and 40 people were injured, with women and children being among the victims On 17 October, at approximately 01:00 local time, the Azerbaijani authorities stated that the Armenian Armed Forces had fired Scud missiles at Ganja. Journalists reported three powerful explosions in the city. According to a RIA Novosti correspondent, the missiles struck densely populated residential areas of the city, leveling several rows of residential and other buildings, the first one being less than two kilometers (1.2mi) away from the city hall, and the second within Kapaz District in the east of the city Vasily Polonsky, a "Dozhd" correspondent said that there were no military bases and important objects near the places struck by the missiles. According to local authorities, about 20 houses were destroyed, trapping many civilians under the rubble. Search and rescue teams of the Azerbaijani Ministry of Emergency Situations and servicemen of the Azerbaijani Ministry of Defense arrived at the scene, calling in sniffer dogs to rescue the wounded and recover the dead. Armenia denied responsibility for the attack. As a result of the attack, 13 civilians were killed and more than 52 were injured according to initial reports.
