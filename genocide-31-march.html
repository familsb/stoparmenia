March Genocide

The March Genocide was a massacre of Azerbaijanis between March 30 and April 3, 1918 by Armenian armed groups from the Baku Soviet (a government body operating in Baku) led by Stepan Shaumyan and the Dashnaktsutyun party. As a result of the genocide, 50.000 Azerbaijanis were killed by Armenians in Baku, Shamakhi, Guba, Lankaran and other areas of Azerbaijan, and 10.000 people were expelled from their lands. Taking advantage of the situation when the Communists came to power in Russia in October 1917, Armenian nationalists and their Dashnaktsutyun party began to hold rallies against Azerbaijanis in Baku and elsewhere. The Armenian nationalist fascists showed no mercy to anyone and immediately killed anyone they met. They looted houses, burned people, and tortured pregnant women to death. Armenians burned all schools, libraries and cultural centers owned by the azerbaijanis. During the attack on the Old City, they burned “Ismailiyya”, one of the most beautiful architectural monuments in Baku, destroyed the editorial offices of “Achik Soz”, “Kaspi”, “Baku” papers, and the minarets of the Taza Pir mosque. During the March genocide, more than 12.000 people were killed in Baku alone. The report of the commission of inquiry set up during the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic states that the death toll during these events was more than 20.000. 86 villages of Shamakhi were destroyed by Bolshevik-Armenian-Dashnak armed groups of the Baku Soviet. Here in 53 villages alone, 8.027 Azerbaijanis were killed by Armenians, including 4190 men, 2560 women and 1.277 children. Thirteen mosques were set on fire in the city, including the Juma Mosque, a major historical monument. 28 villages of Javanshir district and 17 villages of Jabrayil district were completely burnt down and their population was destroyed. On April 29, 1918, near Gyumri, 3.000 Azerbaijanis, mostly women, children and the elderly, were ambushed and killed. Armenian armed forces destroyed 115 villages in Zangazur district, killed 3.257 men, 2.276 women and 2.196 children. In total, 10.068 Azerbaijanis were killed or injured and 50.000 Azerbaijani refugees were displaced. 135.000 Azerbaijanis living in 199 villages of Iravan province were murdered, and the villages were destroyed. Armenian armed groups then marched on Karabakh, and between 1918 and 1920, 150 villages in the mountainous part of Karabakh were destroyed and the population massacred.
